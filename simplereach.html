Husband-and-wife team Josh Loeb and Zoe Nathan run Los Angeles’ Huckleberry Bakery and Café. The Santa Monica natives founded the popular place, which is always filled with fresh baked goods that are ripe for eating in the ever-sunny Southern California weather. With the café’s location one mile away from the beach, it seems like it’d always be summertime for Huckleberry—but the seasons do change, which is where this Lemon-Pistachio Cake comes in. The warm, sweet dish is a delicious way to brighten up chillier mornings.

“This is one of my go-to winter breakfast cakes, when there is almost no fruit to cook with and I’m getting a little tired of lemon by itself,” says Nathan in their cookbook Huckleberry: Stories, Secrets, and Recipes from Our Kitchen. “This cake is buttery, but the richness is cut by its strong lemon flavor and the nuttiness of the toasted pistachios.”

Although there’s a sweet kick in this recipe, there’s a nice healthy bonus, too: California pistachios are packed with protein, good for your heart, and one serving of nuts can give you more than 10% of the recommended daily value of dietary fiber and essential vitamins and minerals. And if cake is the way to get vitamins, then sign us up.

Huckleberry’s Lemon-Pistachio Cake is perfect for cooler weather since California pistachios are harvested in the fall, so fits in with Loeb and Nathan’s commitment to using the freshest local ingredients, even as the seasons change. While you’re cooking up this treat in the drearier months, know you’re bringing a little piece of beautiful California into your home—not only because the recipe comes from a well-known Los Angeles bakery, but also because a vast majority of pistachios grown in the United States come from California, like Alison Nagatani’s farm in Earlimart.

So, ready to hit the kitchen? Before you bake, heed Nathan’s tips to make sure you get the most out of your fresh nuts on this wintertime cake. “It is very important to toast your pistachios to a golden brown to get that rich flavor.” She adds, “Another integral part of this recipe is not chopping the nuts for the topping too finely: if I could ask you to cut each nut in half and not sound like a total jerk I would, because the larger pieces on top of the cake make it look rustic and beautiful.” Not to mention totally delicious.

Directions

STEP 1
To make the cake, position a rack in the middle of your oven and preheat to 350°F. Line and grease a 10-inch round cake pan.

STEP 2
In a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream the butter, sugar, salt, and lemon zest on medium-high speed, until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Incorporate the vanilla and eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Be sure to scrape the sides of the bowl well. Pause mixing and add the California pistachios, almond meal, flour, and baking powder on low speed. Mix cautiously, just until incorporated.

STEP 3
Scoop the batter into the prepared pan. Bake for 1 hour and 5 min­utes, or until a cake tester comes out clean. Do not overbake! Allow to cool for about 15 minutes in the pan.

STEP 4
While the cake is cooling, make the glaze: Simmer the California pistachios, sugar, and lemon juice together until the sugar is completely dissolved and the pistachios are nicely coated, about 2 minutes. Set aside.

STEP 5
Place a flat plate on top of the cake and pan. Carefully invert the cake onto the plate by flipping both upside down. Then lift the pan off the cake. Gently pull the parchment from every nook and cranny of the cake, being careful not to break the cake. Rest your serving plate on the bottom of the cake and turn the cake right-side up onto the plate.

This cake is best served the day it’s made but keeps, tightly wrapped, at room temperature, for up to 3 days. You can serve this cake with lightly sweetened whipped cream or not; it can completely stand on its own.
